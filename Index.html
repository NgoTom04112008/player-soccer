Best player of the world!

Cristiano Ronaldo is a Portuguese professional footballer widely regarded as one of the greatest players in the history of the sport. Born on February 5, 1985, in Madeira, Portugal, he began his senior career with Sporting CP before moving to Manchester United in 2003. There, he gained global attention with his extraordinary skill, speed, and goal-scoring ability. Ronaldo later joined Real Madrid in 2009, where he became the club's all-time leading scorer and won numerous titles, including four UEFA Champions League trophies. After successful spells with Juventus and a return to Manchester United, he moved to Al-Nassr in Saudi Arabia. Renowned for his athleticism, work ethic, and competitive spirit, Ronaldo has won multiple Ballon d'Or awards and remains an iconic figure in world football both on and off the pitch.

Soccer—called football in most of the world—is the most popular sport globally. It's a team sport played between two teams of 11 players on a rectangular field with a goal at each end. The objective is simple: score more goals than the opponent by getting the ball into the opposing team’s net. Key Features: The Ball: Round, typically size 5 for adults. Match Duration: Two 45-minute halves with a 15-minute halftime break. No Hands: Players (except the goalkeeper) can't use their hands or arms to touch the ball during play. Offside Rule: A player is offside if they are nearer to the opponent’s goal than both the ball and the second-last defender when the ball is passed to them, under certain conditions. Fouls and Free Kicks: Infractions result in free kicks or penalties depending on the severity and location. Yellow/Red Cards: Used to discipline players—two yellow cards equal a red (ejection), and a straight red also leads to ejection. Popular Tournaments: FIFA World Cup: Held every 4 years, it's the most prestigious tournament. UEFA Champions League: Features top European club teams. Premier League (England), La Liga (Spain), Serie A (Italy), and others are major domestic leagues. Skills & Strategy: Soccer involves a mix of physical stamina, technical skill (like dribbling, passing, and shooting), tactical awareness, and teamwork. Positions range from defenders and midfielders to forwards and goalkeepers, each with unique responsibilities.
